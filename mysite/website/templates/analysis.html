Restaurant Analysis

Relationship between time period(weekday), customer flow and avg rating

At first, we divide the restaurants into popular restaurants which have large amount of reviews, and not popular restaurants which have small amount of reviews.

One example for not popular restaurant:

name: International House of Pancakes , # of review: 31

So from the not popular restaurant example which has fewer number of reviews, we find that at Fridays, the restaurant has smaller customer flow along with higher average rating , then at Saturdays, while the customer flow apparently increase, the average rating decrease obviously.

Another example for popular restaurant:

name: Bellagio Hotel , # of review: 2270

(for those popular restaurant, we may need to magnify the difference of avg ratings)

Then from the popular restaurant example which has large number of reviews, we find that because of the larger amount of reviews, the average ratings for each weekday are approximate, so we need to magnify the difference between average ratings, then after magnification, we could discover the similar situation, such that the cutomer flows at workdays are smaller than at weekends, but the average ratings at workdays are bigger than at weekends.

Therefore, for both popular and not popular restaurants, we could find similar situations that when the restaurant has bigger customer flow, the average ratings may decrease in most times.To some degree, we could assume that the average rating will be inversely proportional to the customer flow.

Next, we try to check our assumption by randomly choosing and analyzing 500 restaurants, which half is popular and the other half is not popular restaurants. We check the slope of the fitting curve we get to prove the assumption (If the value of slope is negative, then it proves that the relation is inversely proportional).

In the plot, we could find that most of the points have slopes that is smaller than 0, which proves our finding that the average rating will be inversely proportional to the customer flow.

The density plot of the above data looks like:

So the slopes have largest frequency in range [-0.05,0], and few avg slopes are bigger than 0 which proves that at most times, on average, when the restaurant has bigger customer flow, the average ratings may decrease. In conclusion, the average rating will be inversely proportional to the customer flow at most cases.
